# Contributor Agreement (CLA)

By submitting a contribution to wikipath you agree to the terms below.
For Tier-0 anonymous "Suggest" contributions (no account), agreement is
implicit through the act of submission and is bound to the more limited
scope in §3.

## 1. Your contribution

A "contribution" is any text, fact, relation, source citation, name
variant, image, code, documentation, translation, or other material you
submit to the project through:

- The Suggest form (Tier 0, anonymous, no account).
- The Edit / Add UI (Tier 1, requires authentication and CLA acceptance).
- A pull request to https://github.com/start01/wikipath.
- A direct submission to the maintainer.

## 2. You confirm that

- You have the legal right to submit the contribution.
- The contribution does not infringe third-party copyright, trademark,
  or privacy rights. Wikipedia / Wikidata derived facts cited in good
  faith are within scope.
- For photographs you submit, you either own them or have explicit
  permission from the rights holder.
- For records about living persons, you have either the person's
  consent or the record contains only information already published in
  independent sources.
- You will follow the [Code of Conduct](CODE-OF-CONDUCT.md).

## 3. License grant (Tier 0 — anonymous Suggest)

You grant wikipath a perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free,
non-exclusive license to use, reproduce, modify, adapt, publish, and
distribute your suggestion as part of the wikipath database. The
project may release your suggestion under the database license (see
LICENSE-DATA).

You do not waive any moral rights or trademark rights.

You retain copyright in any photograph or quotable text you submit; the
license above is limited to inclusion in the wikipath database.

## 4. License grant (Tier 1 — authenticated Edit / Add)

In addition to §3, you grant wikipath the right to **re-license your
contribution** under a future license that is at least as permissive as
the current LICENSE-DATA. This allows the project to evolve its database
license terms without re-acquiring permission from every past
contributor. The maintainer will not switch to a more restrictive license
without explicit notice to the contributor base.

## 5. License grant (code / docs)

For contributions to the code or documentation files (anything outside
the `data/` or `internal/schema/` directories), your contribution is
licensed under the MIT License — see LICENSE.

## 6. No representations beyond §2

The project accepts your contribution AS-IS. You make no warranty about
fitness for any purpose. The project's accuracy disclaimer (see TERMS.md)
applies to your contribution as well.

## 7. Disagreements

If a contribution is found to violate §2 (e.g. you didn't have the right
to submit it), the maintainer will remove it. If you wish to withdraw a
past contribution, contact the takedown channel; we will soft-delete
within 7 days and hard-delete within 30 days, subject to the
non-revocability clauses above for contributions already incorporated
into derivative databases by third parties.

## 8. Signing

- **Tier 0**: submitting the Suggest form constitutes acceptance.
- **Tier 1**: clicking "I accept" on first sign-in records a
  `cla_signed_at` timestamp against your `contributor` row.
- **GitHub PR**: opening a PR with the line
  `Signed-off-by: Your Name <you@example.com>` in the commit trailer
  constitutes acceptance.
